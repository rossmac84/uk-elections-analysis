Political Glossary

Swing: The average change in vote share between two parties. A 5% swing means one party gained 5% while the other lost 5%, representing a 10-point gap change.
Valence Politics: Elections decided by which party voters believe is more competent at delivering shared goals (good economy, good NHS) rather than which party has better policies.
Class Dealignment: The weakening relationship between social class and voting behaviour. Working-class voters no longer automatically vote Labour; middle-class voters no longer automatically vote Conservative.
Hegemony: When one ideological worldview becomes "common sense." Thatcherism achieved hegemony 1979-1997.
Red Wall: Labour constituencies in the North and Midlands that voted Leave in 2016 and Conservative in 2019. Many returned to Labour in 2024.
Third Way: Tony Blair's ideological position combining market economics with social investment.
Neoliberalism: Economic philosophy emphasising free markets, privatisation, deregulation. Associated with Thatcher.
Populism: Political style contrasting "pure people" vs "corrupt elite." Johnson's 2019 campaign used populist rhetoric.
UKIP (UK Independence Party): Eurosceptic party founded 1993. Won 12.6% in 2015, forced Cameron to promise EU referendum. Collapsed after Brexit achieved. Succeeded by Brexit Party (2019) then Reform UK (2024).
Brexit Party: Founded by Nigel Farage in 2019 to deliver Brexit. Won 2.0% in 2019 election after standing down in Conservative-held seats. Rebranded as Reform UK in 2021.
Reform UK: Continuation of Brexit Party. Won 14.3% (4.1m votes) in 2024 but only 5 seats due to FPTP. Split Conservative vote decisively, helping Labour to landslide.
FPTP (First Past the Post): UK's electoral system where candidate with most votes in each constituency wins, even without majority. Creates disproportionate results - Reform UK won 14.3% of votes but 0.8% of seats in 2024.
Tactical Voting: Voting for a candidate not because they're your first choice, but to prevent another party from winning. Highly effective in 2024 with anti-Conservative coordination between Labour and Lib Dem voters.
